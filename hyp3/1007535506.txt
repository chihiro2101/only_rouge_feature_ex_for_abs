the legal thriller usually starts its business with the court proceedings following the closure of an investigation , often resulting in a new angle on the investigation , so as to bring about a final outcome different from the one originally devised by the investigators some of these stories are whodunits ; in others , the criminal is well known , and it is a case of getting enough evidence very often , no actual mystery even existed : the books simply revolved around justice being served to those who deserved harsh treatment , which was described in explicit detail following other conventions of classic detective fiction , the reader is normally presented with the puzzle and all of the clues , and is encouraged to solve the mystery before the solution is revealed in a dramatic climax
